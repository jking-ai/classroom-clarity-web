Based on the uploaded document, the primary causes of the French Revolution were a combination of social inequality, economic hardship, and political ineptitude.

  • Social Inequality: The Estate System created deep resentment. The Third Estate (commoners) paid nearly all taxes while having no political power, whereas the clergy and nobility were exempt .
  • Economic Crisis: Years of deficit spending, costly wars (including aiding the American Revolution), and bad harvests led to soaring bread prices and starvation .
  • Weak Leadership: Louis XVI was indecisive and failed to implement necessary financial reforms proposed by his advisors like Jacques Necker .
